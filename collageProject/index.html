ABOUT SHRI RAM GROUP

Shri Ram Group was established on 9th July in 2001 and since its first step, it continued to remain the best engineering college in Jabalpur, MP in India. Dr. Shiv Prasad Kosta, recognized as a renowned Educationist and an Electronics Scientist is the head of the educational group. All of the institutes set up under the group are recognized as best engineering college in Jabalpur, MP in India.

Shri Ram Institute of Technology recently has prestigious ranks in the Times Engineering Institute Ranking Survey 2019 conducted by Times of India. The institute secured 2nd rank among Top private engineering institutes in central zone.

Shri Ram Institute of Technology also has prestigious ranks in the DQ-CMR Survey 2022 conducted by DATAQUEST. The institute secured 1st rank among All private engineering institutes in Jabalpur and 3rd rank among Top private T-schools 2019 in Madhya Pradesh.

The Institute currently listed in the AAA category of Engineering Institutes per the survey conducted by Careers 360.

Shri Ram Group has large numbers highly qualified faculty members to deliver curriculum that aims at development of courses and programs based on both academic and industrial inputs. Whether you look for performance in B.Tech., M.E./M.Tech, MBA and MCA, Pharmacy, Commerce and Law courses, you will find top results of Shri Ram Group’s students. Especially, every year, the institute witnessed large numbers of merit holders in Bachelor of Technology .

Shri Ram Group has consistently maintained its strong position among various other engineering colleges in India by providing best placements to its students. Until now, students of the educational group not only get placements in MNCs, but also in defense and civil services. Along with quality education and placements, students pursuing their courses in the institute will be able to receive excellent facilities, like emblematic architecture, IT-enabled campus, vast, green and lush area, properly equipped lab facilities, smart classrooms and many more.
